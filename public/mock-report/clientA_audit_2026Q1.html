01 · Executive summary

The system is healthier than last year. Two critical risks remain.

Tourvest's Sage X3 environment scored 74 of 100 in Y1, a 12-point improvement over the Y0 baseline of 62. Severe-risk count fell from 11 to 6, and an estimated R 240K of annual leakage was closed. Two critical control gaps persist and warrant Q3 action.

74
+12 vs Y0 · Target 80

System Health Score

Composite of four pillars — Controls, Data Quality, Leakage Reduction, and Efficiency. The score reflects the audited environment as of 12 April 2026 and is calibrated against the Sage X3 reference benchmark for travel-services operators.

What changed

Five severe risks closed since the Y0 baseline, including the most acute SoD conflict in payroll, the GL duplicate-journal issue, and the vendor-master orphan-record class. Two new findings emerged in the reporting layer (cached overnight data) and the FX revaluation schedule — both are low-to-medium impact but warrant action this cycle.

03 · Top-10 risk register

Ranked by financial impact

Each finding includes a quantified financial impact (where modellable), severity classification, recommended fix, and proposed owner.

01

Segregation of duties conflict in AP module

Critical Category: Controls Owner: Finance Manager

Three users hold both invoice-creation and invoice-approval permissions in the AP module. Recommended fix: split roles and enforce via a workflow lock that rejects same-user approval.

ImpactR 180K
02

Duplicate vendor records with shared bank details

Critical Category: Data quality Owner: AP Lead

Seven duplicate vendor records identified by matching bank account numbers. Add a uniqueness constraint on the bank-details field and merge existing duplicates.

ImpactR 95K
03

PO approval thresholds bypassed via journal entries

High Category: Controls Owner: Finance Manager

Journals over R50K were being booked directly without secondary approval. Add a workflow lock and audit trailing 12 months for back-dated entries.

ImpactR 68K
04

Dormant user accounts with active permissions

High Category: Controls Owner: IT Lead

Eighteen user accounts inactive for more than 90 days retain active module permissions. Deactivate and schedule a quarterly access-review workflow.

Impact—
05

GL account 4100 used for multiple expense categories

High Category: Data quality Owner: Finance Manager

Account 4100 mixes Travel, Marketing, and Office expenses. Split into dedicated child accounts and reclassify the trailing 12 months.

ImpactR 42K
06

Invoice payment terms inconsistent across vendors

High Category: Efficiency Owner: AP Lead

Vendor master shows 12 distinct payment-term configurations across the active book. Standardise on Net 30 unless contractually overridden.

ImpactR 38K
07

PO receipt confirmations missing on 12% of POs

Medium Category: Efficiency Owner: Procurement

Twelve percent of POs lack receipt confirmation beyond the 14-day window. Auto-reminder workflow and 30-day escalation to procurement lead.

ImpactR 22K
08

Foreign currency revaluation runs intermittently

Medium Category: Data quality Owner: Finance Manager

FX revaluation was skipped in 4 of the last 12 month-end closes. Make it a mandatory workflow step.

ImpactR 18K
09

User training records not linked to access provisioning

Medium Category: Controls Owner: IT Lead

05 · Process issues

Workflow failures observed

Non-quantified findings that influenced the score but didn't model directly into leakage.

Month-end close averages 9.2 days Efficiency

Above the Sage X3 reference benchmark of 6 days. Bottleneck identified in manual AP reconciliation and the FX revaluation gap (finding 08).

AP invoice cycle uses 4 workarounds outside Sage X3 Workaround

Two Excel-based reconciliation templates and two email-based approval routes operate in parallel to the system. Each is a control gap and an audit-trail gap.

Reporting layer has 6 dashboards reading from the same overnight cache Architecture

Centralising to a single live read-replica would close finding 10 and reduce data-staleness complaints from the executive team.

Methodology · Scope · Contact

Audit performed using the GAS Anomaly engine v2.4.1 over five working days, ingesting five Sage X3 exports (GL, AP, PO, Users, Workflows) covering the 12-month window 01 Apr 2025 — 31 Mar 2026. Sampling methodology: full population for transaction-level findings, stratified sampling for behavioural-pattern findings (1% sample, 95% CI). Health-score model: equally-weighted four-pillar composite calibrated against the Sage X3 travel-services reference benchmark.

Questions or to contest a finding: respond inside the portal via "Ask Jera about this finding" on any risk-detail screen.
